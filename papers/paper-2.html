Scalable Algorithms for Large-Scale Data Visualization

Published: 2023

Venue: IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics

Authors: Nikolaj Storm Petersen, Dr. Sarah Kim, Prof. Michael Rodriguez

Abstract

We introduce new algorithms for rendering complex datasets in real-time, enabling interactive exploration of big data. Our approach combines hierarchical data structures with GPU-accelerated rendering techniques to achieve scalable visualization performance for datasets containing millions of data points.

The proposed algorithms demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods, maintaining interactive frame rates even with datasets exceeding 10 million points. We validate our approach through comprehensive benchmarks and real-world case studies in scientific computing, business intelligence, and social network analysis.

Key Contributions

  • Novel hierarchical data structures optimized for GPU rendering
  • Adaptive level-of-detail algorithms for large-scale visualizations
  • Performance evaluation across multiple domains and dataset types
  • Open-source implementation with comprehensive documentation

Citation

Petersen, N. S., Kim, S., & Rodriguez, M. (2023). Scalable Algorithms for Large-Scale Data Visualization. IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics, 29(8), 3421-3435. https://doi.org/10.1109/TVCG.2023.1234567
